# AGIF v1 Closed Package Release Note

## What v1 Proves
- AGIF v1 is a runnable local governed multi-cell fabric in a bounded finance document workflow proof domain.
- The closed benchmark package shows deterministic improvement from the flat baseline to the full bounded-adaptation fabric on the six recorded finance cases.
- The closed long-run package includes real `24h` and real `72h` MSI soak evidence, with the machine role kept explicit and the `72h` `WinError 5` bookkeeping caveat kept visible.
- The repo contains a reproducibility package, a claims-to-evidence matrix, a paper-draft status note, and a one-command closure verifier.

## What v1 Does Not Prove
- MacBook Air-only long-run endurance.
- AGI, broad open-world generality, or unrestricted self-improvement.
- Organic near-capacity split or merge usefulness under normal load.
- Multi-domain transfer or second-domain proof beyond finance.

## What Remains For Post-v1 Extension Work
- Organic split or merge proof under normal near-capacity load.
- Governed skill-graph and transfer-approval proof.
- A second bounded proof domain with causal cross-domain transfer evidence.
- A revised paper or addendum aligned to any new extension evidence.

## What Was Deliberately Excluded From v1 Scope And Why
- MacBook-only long-run endurance:
  - v1 closed on one honest long-run soak machine, MSI, while keeping MacBook Air as the development, documentation, benchmark, and primary target machine.
- Multi-domain transfer:
  - v1 froze the proof boundary to finance so the first software proof stayed bounded, auditable, and small enough to verify end to end.
- Benchmarked organic split or merge:
  - v1 proved lifecycle safety and governed elasticity first through deterministic runtime checks and stress lanes before attempting a larger usefulness benchmark.

These are deliberate scope boundaries for AGIF v1, not hidden failures.
